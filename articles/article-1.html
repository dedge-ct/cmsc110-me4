Tropical storm ‘Nika’ barreling towards eastern Luzon

By: Dennis Gasgonia | ABS-CBN News

Typhoon Marce as of 10:00 a.m. on November 5, 2024.

Tropical cyclone Nika has continued to intensify and may turn into a severe tropical storm on Sunday, the state weather bureau said Saturday. The tropical storm was last sighted 625 kilometers east of Virac, Catanduanes at 10 p.m.

It carries maximum sustained winds of 75 kilomters per hour near the center, gusts of up to 90 kph, and is moving westward at 20 kph.It will intensify further into a severe tropical storm before making landfall over Aurora or Isabela on Monday afternoon or evening. The possibility of rapid intensification into a typhoon is not ruled out.

Heavy to intense rains and strong winds will batter Cagayan Valley, Cordillera, Ilocos Region and parts of Central Luzon as the storm crosses Northern Luzon through early Tuesday.

Serious and widespread flooding and landslides are expected.

Nika may exit the Philippine area of responsibility Tuesday evening, according to PAGASA.
