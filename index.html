Tropical storm ‘Nika’ barreling towards eastern Luzon
Headline | By: Dennis Gasgonia

Tropical cyclone Nika has continued to intensify and may turn into a severe tropical storm on Sunday, the state weather bureau said Saturday. The tropical storm was last sighted 625 kilometers east of Virac, Catanduanes at 10 p.m. It carries maximum sustained winds of 75 kilomters per hour near the center, gusts of up to 90 kph, and is moving westward at 20 kph.

President Ferdinand Marcos Jr on Monday assured that his administration is committed to strengthening its disaster risk reduction and response efforts to prevent the further loss of lives amid the worsening effects of climate change.

UP scientists lauded for topping AD Scientist Index 2025
Featured | By: Stephanie Sevillano

The University of the Philippines (UP) lauded its scientists on Monday after they topped other Philippine higher education institutions in the Alper-Doger (AD) Scientific Index 2025. The AD Scientific Index is a ranking and analysis system of scientific performance that adds value to the scientific output of individual scientists.

Science | By: Jonathan Garcia

A 60-year-old German man is likely the seventh person to be effectively cured from HIV after receiving a stem cell transplant, doctors have announced.
